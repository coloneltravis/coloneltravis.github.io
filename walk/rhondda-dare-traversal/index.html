Bridging the Rhondda and Dare Valleys

Nov 28, 2015

Starting at Cardiff Central, I took the 10:06 train to Treherbert. After passing through Pontypridd station I noticed when the train entered the Rhondda valley by station names like Trehafod, Porth, Tonypandy and Llwyn-y-pia. The train paused at Dinas Rhondda station as the double-track line reduced to single track. This required the driver to emerge from his cab to enter a small kiosk on the platform where he would phone for a token to allow the train to proceed on to the single track section. It only proved as a reminder to the lack of funding in Wales for a modern railway. A passenger arriving in Wales from London must feel like the Severn Tunnel is some kind of time warp!

The train passed through Treorchy, the home of the famous Male Voice choir. Just one stop at Ynyswen before the train finally reached the end of the track at Treherbert at the head of the Rhondda valley. From here, the track used to continue to loop around to the east to go under the mountains via the Rhonnda Tunnel which linked the Rhondda to the Llynfi valley. The tunnel is now buried and the track removed, although there are hopes that the tunnel can be reopened as a cycle tunnel.

The station at Treherbert was not typical of a terminus station - with only a ticket kiosk and toilets available for passengers. I walked straight on to the street to start walking through the streets of Treherbert. I headed towards Penpych mountain which is distinctive with its flat plateau summit.
